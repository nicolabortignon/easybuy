1. Why should i buy someone's else tracks.


This service is the cheaper in the industry (by far) and is based on the idea that we can all help each other.
Someone can buy your tracks when you need and vice versa.
In order to keep this service going we need people that buy tracks.
If everyone buys tracks when asked to, the service keeps working.
And cherry on top, you also earn money!

2. Is this legal?


It is. Beatport tried to shut down similar services before, unsucessfully.
The truth is that there is no legal infringement.
In case beatport has a different opinion about the topic, our legal team will be happy to refresh them how open trade works.
Most important, all the informations about this system are kept encripted and not shared with 3rd parties. In short, you are safe!

3. What if someone take a picture of my tracks and release publically? This is bad press!


This is a great question. We have been thinking a lot about this as well.
First of all, we vet every single account request, and we let in only people we know first hand.
Let's assume anyway that someone get pissed and decide to vent out on social media. Fine, is not the first case nor the last.
We have a really smart (I can't tell you more) smart way to track that user.
Beside that, there is no much else we can do. My counter question is, if tomorrow a random person fabricates a screenshot of a similar service and start venting on the web against youm what defence would you have? Nothing. Exactly. Luckly enough the web forgets very easily.

4. Can I invite some of my friends?


You can ask them to register, but as said before we will bet every single request, so no offense if we don't allow them in.

5. How do you calculate how to distribute the buys through the time period?


I can write about this for 2 pages. Long story short, we craft an algorithm that receive as input the current chart positions on beatport, and tracks the minimum amount of tracks requested to join the chart.
We have also additional information coming from all the tracks currently in the system.
We match this about "natural buy trends". For instance, on tuesday there are less people on beatport, on saturday is the peak day, and so forth. Mix all this information and you can trust us to distribute your buy in the best way possible, especially over a 2 weeks period.

6. What happen if you can not buy all the tracks that I have requested?


We try to accomodate every request, of course if you ask for 15000 downloads we may have an hard time.
First and foremost we'll let you know straight away while you are trying to order the tracks.
Second, we'll refound every single penny we have not spent!

7. What can I do with the money earned?


You can spend them to get your tracks bought, or , after reaching 50 euro, you can ask to be payed back. We'll send you over the money.

8. Why are you doing this?


We have been in this industry for about a decade now.
We have been the digital autobuy phenomena growing, and we didn't form a strong opinion about it. We just didn't use it originally. We kept investing in traditional form of promotions.
We litterally threw thousands of euro in Blog press, beatport banners, mailing list, and artists charts (yes you can buy slots in djs chart, crazy right? ).
All of this didn't really payed off. Too expansive and not enough impactiful.
We started autobuy, and we saw a really intresting pattern. Tracks that were actually good would stick in the chart for a while after we stopped the push. Tracks that were 'meh', for a lack of a better term, went out of the chart in couple of days.

We believe autobuy is a tool for promoting your tracks, to give that a better expose within all the commercial crap that beatport is throwing in unfair banner promotions.
If your tracks is worthy, autobuy is a good marketing strategy, if your track is ... 'meh' .. it will not last long. Sorry :)

9. Is beatport going to ban my track for 'juicing' ?


No, we have been tinkering the system and there is no way for beatport to track this.

10. Where are the downloads from?


Expect to see a good percentage coming from europe, in a balanced distribution between UK, DE, IT, ES.
Another fair amount will comes from US. The rest is miscillaneaus downloads from the rest of the world with few from MX.
We try to map the downloads to a releastic distribution.
